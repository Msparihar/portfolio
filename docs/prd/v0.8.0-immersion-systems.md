## Overview

These systems transform the portfolio from a themed website into a **living, breathing world** that visitors inhabit. Weather changes, day turns to night, creatures wander, NPCs talk directly to the visitor, and time itself cycles through seasons. The goal: visitors stay for an hour reading books, exploring, and discovering — not just glancing at projects and leaving.

---

## 1. Weather System

Dynamic weather per world, changing on a timer alongside the seasonal/time cycle.

### Elden Ring / Dark Souls
- **Ash storms** — grey particles drifting across the screen, reduced visibility
- **Golden rain** — Erdtree blessing, golden particles falling gently
- **Blood moon** — red tint on everything, phantoms appear more frequently
- **Fog** — thick fog rolls in, only nearby UI elements visible clearly

### Studio Ghibli
- **Rain** — soft animated raindrops, puddle reflections on the desktop
- **Rainbow after rain** — arc appears across the wallpaper after rain stops
- **Cherry blossom wind** — pink petals blowing across the screen (spring)
- **Fireflies** — glowing dots drifting at night (summer evening)
- **Autumn leaves** — orange/red leaves falling (autumn)
- **Soft snow** — gentle, fluffy snowflakes (winter) — different feel from GoT's harsh blizzard
- **Moving scenery** — fields of grass swaying, clouds drifting, characters playing in meadows
- **Ghibli artwork wallpapers** — rotating set of pastoral scenes, watercolor landscapes, characters in fields

### Game of Thrones (per region)
- **The North:** Blizzards (heavy snow + wind), clear frost (still, sparkling ice), aurora borealis
- **King's Landing:** Sunlight and heat shimmer, golden hour glow, occasional thunderstorm
- **Night's Watch:** Perpetual darkness with stars, ice crystal wind, The Wall creaking sounds
- **Dragonstone:** Volcanic ash, ember rain, storm clouds with lightning, lava glow on horizon

### Technical
- CSS animations + particles (canvas or CSS-only for performance)
- Weather state in world store, timer-based transitions
- User can toggle weather on/off in Display Options
- Weather affects creature behavior (creatures hide in storms, come out in calm weather)

## 2. Day/Night Cycle + Time System

### Concept
The desktop wallpaper and ambient lighting shift based on a compressed time cycle. An entire year passes in **1 hour** of real time, so a visitor who stays gets to experience all seasons and times of day.

### Time Mapping (1 hour = 1 year)
- 0-15 min: Spring (dawn → morning → noon)
- 15-30 min: Summer (noon → afternoon → golden hour)
- 30-45 min: Autumn (sunset → twilight → dusk)
- 45-60 min: Winter (night → midnight → pre-dawn)
- Then repeats

### Per World
- **Elden Ring:** Erdtree glows brighter at "noon," fades at night. Moonlight affects rune visibility. Bonfire becomes the only light source at midnight.
- **Ghibli:** Watercolor sky shifts from soft pink dawn to warm gold afternoon to purple twilight to starry night. Forest creatures change with time (birds during day, fireflies at night, owls at dusk).
- **GoT:** Time cycle layered on TOP of seasonal region cycle. North is always colder/darker, King's Landing is always warmer/brighter, but both still have day/night.

### Clock Widget
- Each world has a themed clock in the taskbar or sidebar
- **Elden Ring:** Rune-circle clock with golden hands
- **Ghibli:** Wooden cuckoo clock with leaf hands
- **GoT:** Sundial/hourglass, region-specific style
- Shows both "world time" (compressed) and real time
- Weather icon next to clock shows current conditions

### Technical
- requestAnimationFrame loop or setInterval for smooth transitions
- CSS custom properties for sky color, ambient light, shadow direction
- localStorage tracks elapsed time so returning visitors continue where they left off
- User can speed up/slow down/pause time in Display Options

## 3. Sound Design

**Note: This requires external expertise/assets. Implement last, after visual systems are complete.**

### Per World Ambient Soundscape
- **Elden Ring:** Distant bells, howling wind, bonfire crackling, occasional sword clash, Souls-style choir hum
- **Ghibli:** Birds chirping, wind through leaves, distant piano (Joe Hisaishi-inspired), stream flowing, cicadas at night
- **GoT North:** Howling wind, wolf howl in distance, ice cracking, heavy footsteps in snow
- **GoT King's Landing:** Market chatter (muted), church bells, warm breeze, distant horns
- **GoT Night's Watch:** Dead silence with occasional raven caw, wind against The Wall
- **GoT Dragonstone:** Volcanic rumble, crackling fire, dragon screech (rare), ocean waves

### Interaction Sounds
- Window open/close: themed sound (Elden Ring: stone door, Ghibli: wooden slide, GoT: iron gate)
- Button click: subtle themed feedback
- Boot sequence: full themed audio
- Creature interaction: animal/phantom sound when hovering/clicking
- Seasonal transition: musical sting

### Technical
- Web Audio API for spatial audio and mixing
- Howler.js or Tone.js for cross-browser compatibility
- Volume control and mute toggle in taskbar
- Auto-muted by default (browsers require user interaction before audio)
- Sound files: compressed MP3/OGG, lazy-loaded per world
- **Source:** Freesound.org, Pixabay Audio, or commission from sound designer

## 5. NPC System

### Concept
AI-powered (or scripted) characters that exist within each world. They **break the fourth wall** — they look "out of the screen" at the visitor, acknowledge their presence, and guide them through the portfolio.

### Per World NPCs

**Elden Ring:**
- **The Maiden** — sits at a Site of Grace on the desktop. Speaks in Souls-style cryptic prose. "Ah, another Tarnished... You seek the developer's works? They lie scattered across the Lands Between. Seek the File Manager, and you shall find what remains."
- **Patches** — appears randomly, tries to trick you into clicking things. "There's treasure behind that window... just close your eyes and click."

**Ghibli:**
- **Forest Spirit** — a small Kodama-like creature that appears near the cursor. Waves, points at things you haven't explored. "Have you visited the garden? The projects bloom beautifully there."
- **Cat companion** — follows your cursor lazily. Occasionally speaks: "The author is busy writing. Perhaps you'd like to read their journal?"

**GoT:**
- **The Maester** — appears in a scroll/popup. "My lord/lady, I have prepared a chronicle of the developer's deeds. Shall I guide you to the library?"
- **The Raven** — delivers messages. Flies onto screen, drops a scroll with a tip or lore fragment.

### Fourth Wall Breaking
- NPCs directly address the visitor: "You've been here for 5 minutes now. Have you tried the Terminal?"
- They react to visitor actions: "Ah, you found the hidden codex entry! Well done."
- They acknowledge the time/weather: "Winter comes quickly here. You should read by the fire."
- They suggest actions: "The journals contain the developer's story. You should try them."

### Technical
- Phase 1: Scripted dialogues triggered by conditions (time on site, pages visited, actions taken)
- Phase 2: LLM-powered NPCs that can answer questions about the portfolio in-character (Claude API)
- NPC state tracked in localStorage (which tips they've given, visitor acknowledgment)
- NPCs appear as small animated sprites or styled HTML elements
- Dialogue boxes styled per world (speech bubbles, scrolls, dark souls "YOU DIED" style)

## 6. Mini-games

### Terminal RPG: "Bug Hunter"
- Text-based RPG in the terminal app
- **Enemies:** Named after real code bugs (NullPointerDragon, OffByOneGoblin, RaceConditionPhantom, MemoryLeakSlime)
- **Weapons:** Named after tools/languages (TypeScript Sword +5 type safety, Rust Shield +10 memory safety, Python Staff of rapid prototyping)
- **Battles:** Turn-based, ASCII art enemies, choose attack/defend/use-item
- **XP system:** Level up by defeating bugs, unlock codex entries and achievements
- **Boss fights:** "The Monolith" (legacy codebase), "The Dependency" (node_modules final boss)
- **Easter egg:** Defeating all bosses reveals a secret about the developer

### World-specific Mini-games
- **Elden Ring:** Boss rush mode — fight increasingly difficult "bugs" with Souls-like difficulty
- **Ghibli:** Garden simulator — plant "project seeds" and watch them grow based on real GitHub commit frequency
- **GoT:** Strategy game — defend The Wall against "bug invasions" tower-defense style

### Technical
- Pure terminal-based (text input/output), no extra UI needed
- Game state in localStorage
- Commands: `play`, `attack`, `defend`, `inventory`, `flee`
- Could also have a dedicated "Games" app window for non-terminal games

## 7. Visitor Messages (Soapstone System)

### Concept
Universal across all worlds (but themed per world). Visitors leave short messages on the desktop.

- **Elden Ring:** Orange glowing soapstone messages on the ground
- **Ghibli:** Wooden signposts with messages
- **GoT:** Raven-delivered scrolls

### Features
- Visitors type a short message (max 50 chars) and place it on the desktop
- Messages from other visitors appear as themed markers
- Upvote/downvote system (Souls-style "appraisal")
- Most-upvoted messages persist longer
- Moderation: messages go through a filter + manual approval queue

### Technical
- Backend: simple API (POST message, GET messages, PATCH vote)
- Rate limiting: 1 message per visitor per day
- Storage: database or serverless KV store
- Anti-spam: profanity filter + rate limiting + optional captcha

## 8. World Memory (Returning Visitors)

### Concept
The world remembers you. Returning visitors get personalized greetings and the world reflects their history.

### Greetings
- **First visit:** Full boot sequence, NPC introduction, tutorial hints
- **Return after 1 day:** "Welcome back, Tarnished. The Erdtree still burns." / "The forest remembers you." / "The realm awaits your return, my lord."
- **Return after 1 week:** "It has been long since you walked these halls..." / "The garden missed you. Some flowers have wilted." / "Much has changed in the Seven Kingdoms..."
- **Return after 1 month:** "A ghost from the past... we thought you had gone hollow." / "The spirits whispered you would return." / "The old stories said you'd come back."

### Persistent State
- Last world visited, last page viewed
- Time spent total
- Codex entries discovered
- Achievements earned
- NPC conversation progress
- Weather/season state continues from where they left off

### Technical
- All client-side (localStorage + optional server sync)
- Greeting system checks last visit timestamp on load
- Cookie consent required for persistence (GDPR)

## Notes

- All immersion systems should be toggleable in Display Options
- Performance budget: none of these should slow down the core portfolio experience
- Progressive loading: immersion features load AFTER core content is interactive
- Mobile: most features work, but some (hover-based NPCs, cursor) need mobile alternatives
- Accessibility: all features must have off-switches, no auto-playing audio, respect prefers-reduced-motion
